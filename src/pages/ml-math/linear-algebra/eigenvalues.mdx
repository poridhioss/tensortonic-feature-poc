# Eigenvalues and Eigenvectors

Eigenvalues and eigenvectors are fundamental concepts used in PCA, spectral clustering, and understanding linear transformations.

## Definitions

<Callout type="definition" title="Eigenvector and Eigenvalue">
For a square matrix $\mathbf{A}$, if:
$$\mathbf{A}\mathbf{v} = \lambda\mathbf{v}$$
then $\mathbf{v}$ is an **eigenvector** and $\lambda$ is its corresponding **eigenvalue**.
</Callout>

<Callout type="info" title="Intuition">
Eigenvectors are directions that remain unchanged (except for scaling) when transformed by the matrix. The eigenvalue tells us the scaling factor.
</Callout>

## Finding Eigenvalues

<Formula name="Characteristic Equation">
$$\det(\mathbf{A} - \lambda\mathbf{I}) = 0$$
</Formula>

Solve this equation for $\lambda$ to find the eigenvalues.

## ML Applications

<Callout type="tip" title="Where Eigenvalues Appear">
- **PCA**: Eigenvalues indicate variance explained by each principal component
- **PageRank**: Uses the dominant eigenvector
- **Spectral clustering**: Uses eigenvectors of the Laplacian matrix
- **Stability analysis**: Eigenvalues determine system stability
</Callout>
